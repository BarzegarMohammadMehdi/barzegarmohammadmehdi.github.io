About Me

I am a researcher at the Neuroscience and Neuroengineering Laboratory (NNRL), Iran University of Science and Technology, focusing on the intersection of deep learning and clinical neuroscience.

My work involves designing AI-driven frameworks for early diagnosis of neurological disorders — including epilepsy, Parkinson's disease, schizophrenia, and Alzheimer's disease — using EEG signals. My research integrates explainable AI (XAI), brain connectivity analysis, and source localization to build clinically interpretable models.

I completed my M.Sc. in Neurocognitive Engineering at IUST with a GPA of 18.86/20 (4.0/4.0), graduating as the top student of my intake. My thesis focused on motor imagery hand movement classification using EEG source localization and brain connectivity features.

Always open to new research collaborations and PhD opportunities :)

Research Experience

    • Developed explainable epileptic seizure detection using CNN–Bi-LSTM Attention model on EEG signals (published, JRH 2025).
