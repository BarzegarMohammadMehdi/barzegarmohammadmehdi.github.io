Mohammad Mehdi Barzegar

Neurocognitive Engineer · EEG Researcher · Deep Learning

I am a researcher at the Neuroscience and Neuroengineering Laboratory (NNRL), focusing on the intersection of deep learning and clinical neuroscience. My work involves designing AI-driven frameworks for early diagnosis of neurological disorders including epilepsy, Parkinson's disease, schizophrenia, and Alzheimer's disease using EEG signals.

My research integrates explainable AI (XAI), brain connectivity analysis, and source localization to build clinically interpretable models that bridge the gap between neuroscience and machine learning.

Research Experience 03

Full-Time Researcher

Neuroscience and Neuroengineering Laboratory (NNRL) · Sep 2023 – Sep 2025
  • Developed explainable epileptic seizure detection using CNN–Bi-LSTM Attention hybrid model on EEG signals (published, JRH 2025).
  • Built GCN-based framework for epilepsy diagnosis from EEG functional connectivity graphs (published, IWINAC 2026).
  • Designed UCTESE-Net, an uncertainty-aware Squeeze-and-Excitation network for schizophrenia diagnosis (14 SZ / 14 HC subjects).
  • Built Convolutional SE-Transformer for Parkinson's disease detection from EEG signals.
  • Conducted PRISMA-compliant systematic review and meta-analysis on Alzheimer's diagnosis (~95 studies).
  • Applied eLORETA source localization and PLV/PLI/Coherence features for motor imagery on PhysioNet (109 subjects).
