About Me

I am a researcher at the Neuroscience and Neuroengineering Laboratory (NNRL), Iran University of Science and Technology, focusing on the intersection of deep learning and clinical neuroscience.

My work involves designing AI-driven frameworks for early diagnosis of neurological disorders — including epilepsy, Parkinson's disease, schizophrenia, and Alzheimer's disease — using EEG signals. My research integrates explainable AI (XAI), brain connectivity analysis, and source localization.

I completed my M.Sc. in Neurocognitive Engineering at IUST with a GPA of 18.86/20 (4.0/4.0), graduating as the top student of my intake.

Always open to new research collaborations and PhD opportunities :)

Research Experience

Full-Time Researcher

Neuroscience and Neuroengineering Laboratory (NNRL) · Sep 2023 – Sep 2025

  • Developed explainable epileptic seizure detection using CNN–Bi-LSTM Attention model on EEG signals (published, JRH 2025).
  • Built GCN-based framework for epilepsy diagnosis from EEG functional connectivity graphs (published, IWINAC 2026).
  • Designed UCTESE-Net, uncertainty-aware SE network for schizophrenia diagnosis on public EEG dataset (14 SZ / 14 HC).
  • Built Convolutional SE-Transformer for Parkinson's disease detection from EEG signals.
  • Conducted PRISMA-compliant systematic review and meta-analysis on Alzheimer's diagnosis (~95 studies).
  • Applied eLORETA source localization and PLV/PLI/Coherence features for motor imagery on PhysioNet (109 subjects).
